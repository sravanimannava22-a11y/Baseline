How it works

Know exactly what you have, what you owe, and when.

Baseline is personal financial operations, built in three layers that turn disconnected accounts and deadlines into one automated plan.

The architecture

One plan. Three layers. Every obligation accounted for.

01 / Accounts

Real-time financial position

Plaid-based read-only integration. Balances, recurring outflows, and available funds across all your accounts, updated as things change.

→ Know what you have.
02 / Obligations

Life events, structured

Moves, exams, hobbies, and loans converted into payment timelines with deadlines, splits, and AI contract scanning for contracts and receipts.

→ Know what you owe and when.
03 / FP&A

Plan, forecast, coordinate

Monthly savings targets, shortfall alerts before deadlines are breached, and shared workspaces that let you plan with roommates or partners without a joint account.

→ Coordinate without sharing a bank account.
Why Baseline

Other tools explain the past. Baseline prepares you for what's next.

Budgeting apps track spending that already happened. Baseline is forward-looking by design, built around the deadlines and life events you need to actually plan around.

The old way

Existing tools

  • ✕ Backward-looking only, tracks past spending.
  • ✕ No deadline math or shortfall alerts.
  • ✕ No coordination layer for shared obligations.

  • ✕ Net worth dashboards that don't help you decide.

Personal FP&A

  • ✓ Forward-looking obligation modeling against fixed deadlines.
